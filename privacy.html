Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2025

Overview

MCP to Skill allows you to connect MCP (Model Context Protocol) servers to OpenClaw. When you use OAuth to connect services like Notion, we exchange authorization codes for access tokens and store them briefly to enable your skill setup.

Data We Process

OAuth tokens: Access tokens obtained via OAuth are stored temporarily in a secure key-value store to complete the connection flow. They are used only to generate the CLI command for your OpenClaw environment and are not retained long-term.

Redirect URLs and state: OAuth state values are stored briefly during the authorization flow to prevent CSRF and are deleted after use.

Third-Party Services

OAuth is handled by each provider (Notion). Their respective privacy policies apply to data you share with them during authorization.

Contact

For privacy-related questions, open an issue at github.com/filiksyos/mcptoskill.
